Part 1: Medicine programme in UBD, Brunei Darussalam?

July 6, 2017 in UBD Medicine

This is part 1 of the post regarding the medicine programme that is available in Universiti Brunei Darusalam. All these information are correct. They are based on author experience and are also gathered from the main website.

This post will be updated if the contents are outdated. Please do contact the author if there are any mistake or problem in the post.

Most people (including local people) doesn’t even know that UBD is offering Medicine programme (Medical Doctor), and they also look down upon the programme due to the fact it’s from “BRUNEI”.

I hope this post will be useful to all readers and may help clear your thoughts about studying Medicine in Brunei. If you have any questions, please do contact me through my email.

Tl;dr

  • Studying Medicine in UBD is great!
  • You get to study abroad too.
  • 4 factors affecting your selection for foreign universities.

Medicine is a twinning programme under PAPRSB’s Institute of Health Science (IHS), where there’s no bell curve distribution marking unlike other courses available in UBD. With a high requirement of 320 A-level points(1A2B) and a maximum 30 students only(can be more), the selection process is intriguing as you have to undergo multiple mini interview(10 stations).

Currently IHS are offering 5 degrees including:

  1. Medicine

  2. Dentistry (Starting August 2017)

  3. Pharmacy

  4. Biomedical Science

  5. Nursing and Midwifery

What do you mean by twinning programme?

Basically, you study 3 years in Brunei Darussalam and another 3⁄4 years abroad in your chosen Partner Medical School to complete your studies.

It can also be considered as a Double Degree programme. Because you will get BHSc (Medicine) after studying for 3 years in Brunei and MBBS/MD after 3⁄4 years studying aboard.

Partner Medical School (PMS)? Huh?

Foreign Medical schools that have signed a Memorandum of Understanding or made a contract with UBD, so they are able to send UBD students to complete their studies to get the license to practise as a medical officer.

In the end of your 2nd year, a fixed list of PMS will be available for you to select from. Each year, the list shall be different. This could be due to the amount of students in your cohort or the quota of the PMS are met.

List of available PMS (Subject to change):

  • University of Glasgow, UK

  • University of Aberdeen, UK

  • King’s College London, UK

  • St. George’s University of London, UK

  • University of Southampton, UK

  • University College Cork, Ireland

  • Trinity College of Dublin, Ireland

  • National University of Ireland (Galway), Ireland

  • University of Tasmania, Aus

  • University of Adelaide, Aus

  • University of New South Wales, Aus

  • Australian National University (Canberra), Aus

  • Chinese University of HongKong, HK

  • University of Otago, NZ

Factors affecting your PMS Selection:

  • Quotas

  • 4th semester cGPA

  • IELTS result

  • Brunei Citizenship

Each PMS has different requirements and most can only accept approximately 1-2 students. Some of the listed universities also requires you to have a minimum cGPA of 3.2⁄5.0 which can be easily achieved if you’ve passed or achieved grade C in all your subject modules. Your citizenship is also another factor in selecting your PMS, some of the universities are only available for Brunei Government Scholars only. These would mean that you are required to undergo MOE interview to get your scholarship; a MIB interview in malay language, but you will mostly likely be given the scholarship.

Since each university has a quota, it’s best to do research (Teaching styles, Topics taught) on your selected university and discuss with your course-mates. If there are a lot of people wanting that single university, you will have to compete for it with your cGPA. The higher your cGPA, the higher your chance to get into that PMS.

Beside that, all of the PMS requires you to take IELTS which cost about BND320(Price may increase in the future) and if you didn’t meet the IELTS requirement, you have to take it again.

IELTS Requirements (Overall required results based on country)

  • United Kingdom = Overall band of 7.0; Each element 7.0 or higher.

  • Ireland = Overall band of 7.0

  • Australia = Overall band of 6.5 or 7.0

  • HongKong = Overall band of 6.0

Multiple Mini Interview

Congratulations! If you are called to conduct the interview, you are one step closer to entering Institute of Health Science (IHS).
